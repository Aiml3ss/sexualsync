Get curious. Get in sync.

A private, mobile-first room for couples to talk about what they want. This is a short walkthrough of the thinking behind it and how it works once two people are inside.

Premise

Some conversations are easier to start in writing.

Sexualsync gives two adults a private room to ask clearly, share limits, save ideas, and find overlap before the conversation has to happen out loud.

The core idea is simple: make the first sentence easier, keep the room private, and leave space for yes, no, counter, or "tell me later" without pressure.

The premise that survived

Some things are easier to type than say.

Not because the people don't want to talk. Because the cold start is hard. Sexualsync removes the cold start.

The shape

One room. Two people. Nothing else.

No feed. No discovery. No followers. No directory.

One partner creates the room and sends an invite link. The other accepts after Google sign-in. From that point on the room is the only place this content exists. There is no profile to find, no username to search, no public side of the product at all.

Everything you do — every Ask, every limit, every saved fantasy — lives inside that single shared surface. The product has no concept of "the public version of you." It only has the two people who belong in this room.

Inside the room

A small set of pieces that do specific things.

Each piece is built around a different conversation that's hard to start. They're separate on purpose — different rooms of the same house.

Asks

Make the request.

Pick the act, the timing, whether to film, add a note. Send it. Get a yes, a counter, or a pass.

Acts

A shared library.

Start with built-ins, add the words that fit you both. Asks pull from this — so what you call things is yours.

Limits

Lines that stay visible.

Hard no, talk-first, soft, yes-with-conditions. Asks warn or block when they cross one — before anything is sent.

Inspiration

Fantasies, before plans.

A kink, a scene, a what-if. Posting it is your interest signal. Your partner's me too is what makes it mutual.

The Shelf

Save what catches you.

Links, passages, clips, anything worth coming back to. Items open hidden until a partner chooses to reveal them.

The Vault

For just the two of you.

Encrypted clips, encrypted titles, encrypted comments. The server never sees the passphrase. Only you do.

The Pile

Find the overlap.

Both partners privately drop acts. Nothing reveals until both lock in. Only the matches surface.

Blind Reveal

Answer first, see second.

One prompt, both answer in private. Neither answer opens until both are in. No influence either way.

Sext

Just the two of you.

A private message thread. Photos and GIFs encrypt in your browser before they upload — nobody else can read it.

Sex Quiz

Compare the yeses.

Both answer the same deck alone. The result surfaces only where you already agree — never a one-sided want.

Green Lights

What's on the table.

A standing yes / curious / not-now list you each fill in private. Where they overlap becomes a shared menu.

Sexboard

A closer look

Anatomy of an Ask.

The actsPulled from your shared library. Whatever you call it together is what shows up here.
The whenTiming is a first-class field, not an afterthought. "Tonight" and "this weekend" lead to very different yeses.
The filming preferenceSet per Ask. Defaults are easy to change. No ambiguity at the moment.
The noteOptional. Often this is where the actual asking happens — the words that wouldn't come out in person.
The responsesThree paths: yes, counter, or pass. No path is the wrong path.

A note on encryption

Shared-room records — Asks, Acts, Limits, Inspiration, Shelf, activity, feedback — are encrypted before they land in durable storage. If someone got unauthorized database access, they'd see ciphertext, not the contents of the room.

Home view

The Sexboard is the one screen that summarizes the room.

It's tuned for the most common question a couple actually has: "is there anything I need to look at?"

  • Active Asks. What's open, what's awaiting a reply, what's scheduled.
  • Partner activity. Who did what recently, in safe labels — never intimate body copy.
  • Live game status. Open Pile rounds, Blind Reveal prompts ready to reveal.
  • Recent overlap. Things you both said yes to in the last little while.
  • Anything waiting on you. The single most important panel — surfaces what you owe a response to.

Design rule

The board tells you the state of the room without describing the state of the room.

A status pill that says "they want your take" tells you everything you need to know about urgency, and nothing about the act involved.

Built-in safety

Limits are part of the grammar, not a separate page.

A limit isn't a setting buried in preferences. It's saved alongside the Acts library, and every Ask is checked against it before it can be sent.

Hard no

"filming — hard no." Any Ask that turns filming on is blocked at draft time. The drafter sees the reason, edits, or can't send.

Talk first

"anal — talk first." The Ask still sends, but it carries a badge: this one needs a real conversation before tonight.

Soft limit

"anything before 8am — soft." A warning at send. The drafter has to acknowledge it. The Ask goes through if they do.

Yes — with conditions

"rough — yes, with conditions: lots of warm-up, never first." The conditions surface inline on the recipient's view so they stay in front.

Three places to keep things

Inspiration, the Shelf, and the Vault do different jobs.

A common mistake is to lump "things you save" into one bucket. They're not the same thing — and the app keeps them separated on purpose.

Inspiration

Ideas, before plans.

A fantasy you want to share. A kink to surface. The submission itself is the author's "I'm interested." A partner's "me too" makes it mutual — and only then.

The Shelf

External, opt-in reveal.

A link, a passage, a clip from somewhere else. Items open hidden — your partner chooses when to reveal. Both can react.

The Vault

Yours, end-to-end encrypted.

Clips, titles, comments — all encrypted in the browser with a passphrase the server never receives. Even a database breach can't read it.

The distinction that matters

Inspiration is for the unsaid. The Shelf is for the borrowed. The Vault is for the made. Three different moves, three different surfaces — so a fantasy doesn't get filed next to a recipe you both liked.

How the privacy actually works

Discreet on the outside. Encrypted underneath.

Privacy isn't a setting. It's the shape of the system. Different layers protect different things.

Public surface

Designed not to leak. Marketing pages, emails, push notifications, activity logs, and system messages stay generic. The lock screen never spoils what's inside.

Access

Two people, server-checked. No public feed, no profiles, no discovery. Private routes verify room membership server-side before any data comes back.

Shared-room data

Encrypted at rest. Asks, Acts, Limits, Inspiration, Shelf, activity, presence, push subs, audit logs — encrypted before they touch durable storage.

Private Vault

Encrypted in the browser, with your passphrase. The server never sees the passphrase. Vault media is also not sent to any AI-assisted features.

Your control

Export, sign out, delete. JSON export of profile and room data on demand. Room deletion is scheduled with a seven-day undo window before it purges.
