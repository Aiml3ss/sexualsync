DMCA and Copyright

Copyright removal requests.

Use this page to report copyrighted material that you believe is being stored, linked, or shared through Sexualsync without permission. DMCA safe-harbor handling depends on a complete notice and a registered agent.

Effective May 27, 2026

Copyright contact

Email copyright notices to support@your-host.example with the subject "Sexualsync copyright notice". (Operator: replace this placeholder with your own contact address.)

Important owner task: DMCA safe-harbor posture requires designating an agent with the U.S. Copyright Office and keeping the public agent record current. This page creates a public intake path, but registration should be completed separately.

What to include

  • Your legal name, organization if any, and contact email.
  • A physical or mailing address and phone number for the claimant or authorized agent.
  • A description of the copyrighted work you claim was infringed.
  • The URL, invite link, workspace name, screenshot, or other information reasonably sufficient to locate the material.
  • A statement that you have a good-faith belief the use is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law.
  • A statement that the information in the notice is accurate and, under penalty of perjury, that you are the copyright owner or authorized to act for the owner.
  • Your physical or electronic signature.

Counter-notices

If your content was removed because of a copyright notice and you believe removal was a mistake, email support@your-host.example with the subject "Sexualsync copyright counter-notice". Include enough detail to identify the removed material and explain why you believe it was removed in error.

A lawyer should review any counter-notice before you send it. Counter-notices can have legal consequences.

Safety reports are separate

If the issue involves non-consensual intimate content, minors, exploitation, impersonation, threats, or immediate safety concerns, use the Report and Removal page first.
