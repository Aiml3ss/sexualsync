Report and Removal

Consent is the line.

Use this page to report non-consensual intimate content, minors, exploitation, abuse, impersonation, copyright issues, or other safety concerns involving Sexualsync.

Effective May 22, 2026

Urgent reports

Email hello@sexualsync.io with the subject "Urgent Sexualsync safety report". Include the account email, workspace name if you know it, the link or screenshot needed to locate the issue, and what happened.

If someone is in immediate danger, contact local emergency services first.

Non-consensual intimate content

Sexualsync does not allow non-consensual intimate images, videos, altered media, deepfakes, threats to share intimate content, or intimate content involving minors. Valid removal requests will be reviewed quickly. For non-consensual intimate imagery covered by the TAKE IT DOWN Act, our goal is to remove reported content and known identical copies from Sexualsync-controlled storage within 48 hours of a valid request.

If a platform fails to remove intimate images shared without consent, the FTC also accepts reports at TakeItDown.ftc.gov.

CSAM and minors

Any content involving minors in a sexual context is banned. Reports involving child sexual abuse material or exploitation may be escalated to the appropriate authorities and service providers.

What to include

  • Your name and contact email.
  • The account email or workspace name involved, if known.
  • The URL, invite link, screenshot, or description needed to find the content.
  • Whether you are the depicted person, their authorized representative, a copyright owner, a workspace member, or another reporter.
  • What outcome you are requesting: removal, workspace deletion, account restriction, copyright review, or another safety action.

When possible, we will reply with a confirmation or report reference so the request can be tracked.

What happens next

  1. We acknowledge the report when possible.
  2. We review the report and may ask for more information.
  3. We may remove content, restrict access, freeze a workspace, notify providers, or preserve records if needed for safety or legal reasons.
  4. We share status updates when we can do so without exposing someone else's private data.

Copyright reports

For copyright claims, use the DMCA and Copyright page. For urgent safety issues involving intimate content, use this page first.

Cloudflare reports

Sexualsync runs on Cloudflare. If you need to report abuse directly to Cloudflare, use Cloudflare's official abuse reporting page. Cloudflare has categories for CSAM, non-consensual sexually explicit imagery, copyright, trademark, phishing, malware, threats, and general abuse.
